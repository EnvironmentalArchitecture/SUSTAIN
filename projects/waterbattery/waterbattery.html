INTRODUCTION · WATER BATTERY

Amsterdam Southeast, the Netherlands, 2050,

This project encompasses a battery integrated in an aquifer and a building using water and its potential energy. These kinds of batteries can help with the storage of energy and hence balance out the energy system as there is an imbalance upcoming when we are going to transition to renewable energy resources only. This since the energy supply of renewable resources is not always the same as the demand. Although the efficiency of this specific system is still under assessment it shows a proposal that could possibly be efficient after further assessment has been completed.

Problem Statement

Holland will face several environmental issues in the future as so many other countries will too. First, the issue of an increased flood risk due to the increase of extreme weather circumstances including extremely rainy periods and the rising sea level. Secondly, the salinization of the water due to the rising sea level and the inclination of the soil. And thirdly, an inability to provide energy from renewables at all times due to the fact that the sun does not shine at all times and the wind does not blow evenly at all times.

These environmental issues can cause problems in our electricity supply, drinking water supply and water management. Now we tend to however tackle the problems through singular disciplines. Disregarding a possible integral solution that solves multiple problems at once.

The following project however presents a possible integral solution for the previously stated environmental issues: namely a building and aquifer that can become a water battery.

PROJECT PROPOSAL

The buildings we build could store water. Something that could be a solution for the upcoming flood risks in certain areas of Holland such as Amsterdam Southeast. With this idea the project has not only thought of a way to store water but store its potential energy simultaneously. What if we let the water flow down the water system of the building and its aquifer when we need the energy and up when we need to store the energy. If applied to multiple buildings this could solve a potential energy crisis as the electricity nets are getting overloaded due to the discrepancy between the demanded energy and the available amount of energy with natural resources (see image below).

Moreover, the risk of salinization of the ground water is present. We could use the salinized water for blue energy purposes. As one can generate energy when one let's salinized water flow along fresh water. This way one can let the fresh rainwater flow along the salinized ground water and generate some energy by it. Although this energy potential is minimal the batteries efficiency can be increased with the use of this natural resource.

"The System"

The water flows down the building and the aquifer once the energy is needed and up when the energy is not needed at that specific moment and needs to be stored for later use.
